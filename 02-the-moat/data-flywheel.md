# Data Flywheel Map

Score each loop 1-5. Your weakest loop is where competitors attack first.

## Flywheel Loops

| Loop | What It Measures | Score |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Correction | Do users fix AI outputs? Is that signal captured and reused? | 2/5 |
| Preference | Does the product learn individual / team preferences over time? | 3/5 |
| Domain Context | Does usage in one area improve quality in adjacent areas? | 4/5 |
| Network | Does each new user / team make the product better for everyone? | 1/5 |

---

## Correction Loop - 2/5

### What you capture today
- User feedback on reports
- Accepted/rejected recommendations
- Follow-up questions
- Dashboard interactions

### How it compounds
The AI learns which insights are useful, which alerts are ignored, and which recommendations lead to action. However, there is no automated retraining loop yet.

---

## Preference Loop - 3/5

### What you capture today
- Favorite KPIs
- Reporting frequency
- Preferred dashboards
- Alert thresholds
- Business goals
- Executive vs operational views

### How it compounds
Over time Menza delivers increasingly relevant insights, recommendations, and summaries tailored to each user and team.

---

## Domain Context Loop - 4/5

### What you capture today
- Ecommerce data
- CRM data
- Marketing data
- Finance data
- Operations data
- Customer support data
- Sales data

### How it compounds
Insights generated in one domain improve understanding in others. Marketing performance can be linked to revenue, retention, inventory, support volume, and operational efficiency.

---

## Network Loop - 1/5

### What you capture today
- Limited aggregation across customers
- Minimal shared intelligence between organizations

### How it compounds
Currently, one customer's usage does not significantly improve outcomes for another customer, resulting in weak network effects.

# 90-Day Encroachment Plan

## Your partner played the Big Tech attacker. What was their plan to kill you?

**Attacker:** Microsoft

**Attack Vector (target the weakest loop):**
Commoditize AI analytics and bundle it into existing enterprise products.

### Weeks 1–4 — What they ship

- Copilot-generated executive reports
- Natural language analytics
- KPI anomaly detection
- One-click dashboard summaries

### Weeks 5–8 — How they poach users

- Bundle features into existing contracts
- Free migration tools
- Native integration with Excel, Teams, Power BI, and Dynamics

### Weeks 9–12 — Why users don't come back

- Comparable insights at lower cost
- No additional integration effort
- Existing procurement and security approvals

## Your Defense

1. Become the best AI analyst for business operations, not generic BI.
2. Build proprietary benchmark datasets across industries.
3. Capture outcome signals (which recommendations drive revenue, retention, and efficiency).
4. Create organizational memory that learns how each company operates.
5. Move from insight generation to actionable workflow orchestration and decision support.

### Strategic Takeaway

The biggest risk for Menza AI is being perceived as a thin AI layer on top of existing data platforms. The long-term moat comes from proprietary business context, benchmark intelligence, outcome learning, and organizational memory—not from the LLM itself.
